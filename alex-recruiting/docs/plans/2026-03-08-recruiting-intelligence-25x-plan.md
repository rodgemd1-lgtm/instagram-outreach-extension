# Recruiting Intelligence 25x Plan

Last updated: March 8, 2026

Scope: Jacob Rodgers, Class of 2029 offensive lineman, Pewaukee High School, Wisconsin.

## Executive Summary

- For a Class of 2029 football prospect on March 8, 2026, the highest-leverage work is academic certainty, verified measurables, elite film packaging, disciplined school-list management and fast follow-up. It is not mass messaging.
- Current NCAA football timing matters: Division I football coach-initiated calls, texts, private electronic communication and recruiting materials open on June 15 after sophomore year. For Jacob's class, that date is June 15, 2027.
- Current Division I football official visits open on April 1 of junior year. For Jacob's class, that date is April 1, 2028.
- Division I roster math changed on July 1, 2025 for schools that opt in to the House-settlement model: sport-specific scholarship caps are gone and football now operates under a 105-player roster limit. Inference: "offer quality" is now more school-specific and less standardized than it used to be.
- Division I prospects can discuss and sign third-party commercial NIL deals before enrollment, but deals of $600 or more must be reported on the current NCAA/CSC workflow after enrollment. Inference: NIL is now part of recruiting prep, but compliance and recordkeeping matter as much as opportunity.
- The recruiting family that operates like a disciplined intelligence system will outperform the family that waits for discovery. The right model is: evidence -> priority -> action -> follow-up -> refresh.

## Startup Intelligence OS Frame

Use the recruiting system like an intelligence platform, not just a content app.

- Claims: the facts that matter for recruiting decisions.
- Evidence: source-backed proof tied to each claim.
- Gaps: the missing information that blocks a better decision.
- Opportunities: the actions that become available when a gap closes.
- Cadence: a fixed refresh rhythm so the system does not drift out of date.

Working rule:

- If a recruiting claim changes money, eligibility, visit timing, or coach access, it must have a source and a last-verified date.

## Source-Backed Recruiting Truths

### 1. Academics are a gate, not a bonus

- NCAA Division I requires 16 NCAA-approved core courses, completion of the 10/7 progression before the seventh semester, and at least a 2.3 core-course GPA. [Play Division I Sports](https://www.ncaa.org/academic-standards-initial-eligibility)
- NCAA Division II requires 16 NCAA-approved core courses and at least a 2.2 core-course GPA. [Play Division II Sports](https://www.ncaa.org/sports/2014/10/24/play-division-ii-sports.aspx)
- NCAA core courses must appear on the high school's approved list; post-graduation core-course flexibility differs between Division I and Division II. [Core Courses](https://www.ncaa.org/student-athletes/future/core-courses)
- NAIA entering freshmen can qualify immediately with a 2.30 GPA, or by meeting two of three benchmarks under the current freshman model. [Basics of Freshman Eligibility](https://interpretations.naia.org/basics-of-freshman-eligibility/)

Operational implication:

- Transcript tracking, counselor coordination and approved-course auditing need to live in the same operating system as coach outreach.

### 2. Eligibility Center work should start early

- NCAA says Division I and II-bound athletes need to register with the Eligibility Center and submit official documentation from all high schools/programs attended. [Want to Play College Sports?](https://www.ncaa.org/student-athletes/future)
- NCAA guidance says students should plan to register with the Eligibility Center before ninth grade and make sure final transcripts with proof of graduation are uploaded later. [Submitting a Student's Information](https://www.ncaa.org/sports/2014/12/16/submitting-a-student-s-information)

Operational implication:

- Jacob is already past the ideal starting point, so the practical move is to get the profile and academic artifact workflow in order now rather than waiting for 2027.

### 3. The current Division I football communication window opens on June 15 after sophomore year

- The current Division I manual/report shows the general rule for coach-initiated calls and electronic correspondence beginning June 15 after the prospect's sophomore year in high school. [2025-26 Division I report](https://web3.ncaa.org/lsdbi/reports/getReport/90008)
- NCAA recruiting materials for Division I football follow the same June 15 after sophomore-year milestone for underclassmen in the current model. [2025-26 Division I report](https://web3.ncaa.org/lsdbi/reports/getReport/90008)

For Jacob:

- Earliest practical date for Division I football coach-initiated calls, texts, DMs and recruiting materials: June 15, 2027.

### 4. Official and unofficial visit rules changed and are more favorable than many families think

- Current Division I football official visits begin April 1 of junior year. [2025-26 Division I report](https://web3.ncaa.org/lsdbi/reports/getReport/90008)
- Division I prospects may now take unlimited official visits, but only one per school unless a head coaching change creates an exception. [Official Visits](https://www.ncsasports.org/ncaa-eligibility-center/recruiting-rules/official-visits)
- Schools can pay transportation, lodging, meals and limited game tickets on an official visit. [Official Visits](https://www.ncsasports.org/ncaa-eligibility-center/recruiting-rules/official-visits)
- Unofficial visits are unlimited, and younger prospects can still use them for campus feel and self-scouting even when athletics-department involvement is limited. [Unofficial Visits](https://www.ncsasports.org/ncaa-eligibility-center/recruiting-rules/unofficial-visits)

Operational implication:

- The old "you only get five official visits" advice is stale. The constraint is no longer count alone; it is whether each visit moves a real offer or roster-fit decision.

### 5. NIL is now part of pre-enrollment planning for Division I prospects

- NCAA's April 1, 2025 consent judgment allows prospective Division I student-athletes to discuss, negotiate and sign commercial third-party NIL opportunities before enrollment. [Consent Judgment on NIL Opportunities](https://www.ncaa.org/news/2025/4/1/media-center-consent-judgment-on-name-image-and-likeness-opportunities.aspx)
- NCAA's current NIL page says high school prospects planning to play Division I must report third-party NIL deals of $600 or more made since July 1, 2025 or the start of junior year of high school, whichever is later, within 14 days after starting full-time classes or before the first Division I competition. [NIL (Name, Image, Likeness)](https://www.ncaa.org/sports/2021/7/9/name-image-likeness.aspx)

Operational implication:

- NIL is no longer a post-commitment topic. It belongs in the offer-evaluation file, with compliance recordkeeping attached from day one.

### 6. Roster math changed on July 1, 2025

- NCAA formally adopted Division I roster-limit changes effective July 1, 2025, removing sport-specific scholarship limits for schools that opt in. [DI Board of Directors formally adopts changes to roster limits](https://www.ncaa.org/news/2025/6/23/media-center-di-board-of-directors-formally-adopts-changes-to-roster-limits.aspx)
- The NCAA settlement roster-limit proposal lists football at 105 roster spots. [LSDBi roster limitations proposal](https://web3.ncaa.org/lsdbi/search/proposalView?id=108635)

Inference:

- A school can now distribute aid more flexibly, so "full ride / partial / preferred walk-on" conversations may vary more by school and year. Jacob's system needs offer-package detail, not just offer count.

### 7. Film is still the conversion asset

- Hudl recommends keeping recruiting highlights to 3-5 minutes, leading with the best clips and making the athlete easy to identify. [How to Get Noticed](https://www.hudl.com/support/athlete-recruiting/guides/how-to-get-noticed)
- Hudl also emphasizes updating highlights over time and putting the best material in the first 30 seconds. [How to Get Noticed](https://www.hudl.com/support/athlete-recruiting/guides/how-to-get-noticed)
- NCSA's football recruiting video guide specifically calls out offensive linemen to show good feet and flexibility. [How to Make a Football Recruiting Video](https://www.ncsasports.org/football/recruiting-video)

Operational implication:

- Jacob should maintain both a short recruiter-facing master reel and position-specific OL cutups, with full-game film ready behind them.

### 8. Email still wins as the first-touch channel

- NCSA states email is the best way to introduce yourself to college coaches because it packages the athlete's information cleanly for quick evaluation. [How to Contact College Coaches for Recruiting](https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/contacting-college-coaches)
- NCSA recommends following an introductory email with a phone call after roughly one to three days if there is a reason to do so. [How to Contact College Coaches for Recruiting](https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/contacting-college-coaches)
- If an underclassman does not hear back, that may be a rules issue rather than a lack of interest. [How to Email College Coaches](https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/contacting-college-coaches/email)
- NCSA recommends replying to interested coaches quickly and using structured follow-up rather than random check-ins. [How to Follow up with College Coaches](https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/contacting-college-coaches/follow-up-with-coaches)

Operational implication:

- The app should treat email as the primary outbound channel, X as the awareness layer and phone as the escalation layer once coach response windows open.

### 9. Questionnaires still matter

- Questionnaires add an athlete to the coach's database, put them on the recruiting radar and can be sent before full recruiting contact is allowed. [Recruiting Questionnaires](https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/managing-recruiting-process/recruiting-questionnaires)
- Key fields commonly include measurables, GPA, recruiting video, NCAA ID and social links. [Recruiting Questionnaires](https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/managing-recruiting-process/recruiting-questionnaires)

Operational implication:

- Response speed matters. A filled questionnaire is not admin work; it is a recruiting event.

### 10. Camps and social media are force multipliers only when tied to follow-up

- NCSA recommends contacting coaches before camps and following up after camps so the event is attached to a relationship rather than treated as a one-off. [How to Contact College Coaches for Recruiting](https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/contacting-college-coaches)
- NCSA's current social-media guidance says contacting programs on social is compliant at any time and that consistent, professional activity helps athletes stand out. [How to Use Social Media for College Recruiting](https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/contacting-college-coaches/social-media)
- Football showcases and mega camps create combine-style drill exposure and coach evaluation opportunities. [Find Football Showcases Near You 2026](https://www.ncsasports.org/football/showcases)

Operational implication:

- The camp is not the product. The product is the pre-camp notice, the in-person evaluation, the verified data and the post-camp follow-up.

- Camp and recruiting knowledge bases already exist.

## Gap Register

These are the highest-value gaps left to close.

- Transcript evidence gap: I found GPA references and counselor-facing language, but no first-class artifact pipeline for report cards, official transcripts, approved core-course lists and final graduation proof.
- Rules-verification gap: the repo had stale football timeline logic until this pass. That is a signal that rules claims need a source-backed refresh workflow.
- Offer-package gap: the system tracks schools and recruiting signals, but not the full decision package of aid structure, roster risk, coach stability, NIL posture and depth-chart path.
- Camp ROI gap: the repo has camp data, but not a clear scoring model that ties camp attendance, attending staffs, verified measurables, follow-up and eventual recruiting outcomes together.
- NIL compliance gap: there is no obvious prospect-side NIL ledger in the product for pre-enrollment deals, reporting thresholds and document storage.
- Academic benchmark gap: there is not yet a source-backed "am I trending DI/FCS/DII/NAIA academically and athletically?" dashboard built around real thresholds and evidence.

## The 25x Plan

### Engine 1: Academic Certainty

1. Create a single academic vault with unofficial transcript, report cards, honors and counselor contact info.
2. Pull Pewaukee High School's NCAA-approved core-course list and map Jacob's four-year course path against it.
3. Set internal academic targets above the minimums: 3.3+ preferred, 3.0+ protected floor, zero missing core-course visibility.
4. Register and organize the NCAA Eligibility Center workflow now instead of waiting for the contact window.
5. Add a quarterly academic review that checks GPA trend, course load, test-plan choices and transcript completeness.

### Engine 2: Film and Evaluation Quality

6. Maintain a 3-5 minute master recruiting reel with the best 6-8 plays first.
7. Maintain separate OL cutups for pass protection, run blocking, pulls/climbs and finish/physicality.
8. Tag every game within 24 hours of upload so clips can be repackaged fast for specific coaches.
9. Track verified measurables after every camp: height, weight, wingspan, hand size, shuttle, broad, bench, squat and clean.
10. Keep two full games per season ready to send immediately when a coach asks for more context.

### Engine 3: Relationship Conversion

11. Build a tiered school list: reach, fit and early-opportunity schools.
12. Lead first-touch communication with email, not DMs, unless there is a clear social trigger.
13. Fill every serious questionnaire within 24 hours and follow it with a personalized coach email.
14. Use strict follow-up service levels: same-day response to inbound interest, 48-72 hour post-intro check, then scheduled updates instead of random pings.
15. Keep X professional and active with film, academic updates, camp notes and team-first content.

### Engine 4: Camps, Visits and Market Intelligence

16. Only attend camps that produce at least one of three outputs: verified measurements, staff exposure or specific position evaluation.
17. Email the relevant coach or staff 7-10 days before each camp and include event, position and updated film.
18. Send same-day or next-morning camp follow-up with appreciation, measurable updates and a clean call to action.
19. Use unofficial visits before April 1, 2028 to evaluate campus, academic fit, distance and culture.
20. Use a visit scorecard that grades OL coaching, scheme fit, roster path, academic support, cost and personal comfort.

### Engine 5: Offer Quality, NIL and Decision Advantage

21. Build a school dossier for every serious program: roster age, returning starters, transfer churn, coach tenure, recent OL development and academic fit.
22. Treat every offer as a package: amount, renewability, academic aid, summer aid, housing, books, gray-area conditions and timing pressure.
23. Add an NIL ledger for every deal conversation, signed term, payer, value, deliverable and compliance note.
24. Track comparable Midwest 2029 OL prospects so Jacob's board reflects the real market, not just his inbox.
25. Re-rank the board monthly and cut schools that show no reciprocal interest, poor fit or bad roster math.

## Operating Cadence

Weekly:

- Upload and tag new film.
- Review coach follows, likes, replies and profile views.
- Clear all questionnaire and follow-up tasks.
- Update measurables and training notes if anything changed.

Monthly:

- Re-rank the school board.
- Audit camp schedule and travel ROI.
- Refresh coach/staff changes and roster movement.
- Publish one academic-status snapshot and one recruiting-status snapshot.

Quarterly:

- Re-verify recruiting rule claims that affect contact, visits, NIL or eligibility.
- Review transcript and core-course progress with counselor/family.
- Prune dead targets and add new fits.
- Rebuild the decision criteria based on new information.

Annually:

- Refresh the football recruiting calendar from official NCAA sources.
- Refresh roster-limit, scholarship and NIL process assumptions.
- Re-cut the master reel.

## Startup Intelligence OS Backlog

If this recruiting system is going to outperform a normal family workflow, these are the product gaps to close next.

- Add a source registry for every rules claim with `source_url`, `effective_date`, `last_verified_at`, `sport`, `division` and `confidence`.
- Add a recruiting evidence store for transcripts, core-course lists, counselor uploads, camp measurables and offer documents.
- Add a school-decision model that scores roster opportunity, coaching stability, academics, geography, cost and NIL.
- Add a camp ROI model that scores staff attendance, verified data captured, follow-up sent and downstream recruiting outcomes.
- Add a NIL ledger and reporting checklist for Division I prospect compliance.
- Add an annual rules-refresh workflow so the knowledge layer cannot quietly drift.
