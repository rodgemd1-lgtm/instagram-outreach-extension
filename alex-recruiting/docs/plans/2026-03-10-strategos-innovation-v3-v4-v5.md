## Part 1: Strategos 6-Lens Analysis

### Lens 1: Orthodoxies (False Beliefs)

These are widely held assumptions in recruiting technology that create strategic opportunity for Alex Recruiting.

**Orthodoxy 1: "You need a recruiting service."**
Traditional recruiting services charge $3,000-$15,000/year for manual profile building, mass email blasts, and video editing. The underlying assumption is that human intermediaries create value through relationships and expertise. In reality, most recruiting services provide templated outreach at scale with minimal personalization. An AI-powered platform with genuine intelligence creates more personalized, higher-volume, better-timed outreach than a human recruiter managing 200+ clients simultaneously.

*Strategic opening:* Position Alex Recruiting as the system that replaces the recruiting service, not supplements it. The family should feel they have a recruiting staff, not a subscription.

**Orthodoxy 2: "Highlights are what matter."**
Families spend enormous energy on 3-minute highlight reels -- curated to show only the best plays. Coaches know this and discount them accordingly. What coaches actually want is unedited game film showing every snap. For offensive linemen specifically, coaches evaluate technique across full drives, not isolated pancake blocks.

*Strategic opening:* Build the film intelligence layer that auto-tags full game film, surfaces technique metrics (pad level, hand placement, pull angles), and presents it in a format coaches can evaluate in 90 seconds. This is dramatically more valuable than another highlight reel.

**Orthodoxy 3: "More exposure = better outcomes."**
The recruiting industry operates on the premise that visibility drives offers. NCSA, Rivals, 247Sports all sell exposure as the product. But college coaches do not have an exposure problem -- they have a filtering problem. They are drowning in film and profiles. The real question is not "did the coach see my profile?" but "did the coach see the right information at the right time in the right context?"

*Strategic opening:* Replace exposure-maximization with signal-optimization. The intelligence engine should surface Jacob to the right coaches at the right moment (when roster needs align, when the coaching staff is actively evaluating his position group, when a competing recruit decommits).

**Orthodoxy 4: "Star ratings determine offers."**
Families anchor heavily on Rivals/247Sports/On3 star ratings as validation. In reality, star ratings are backward-looking consensus indicators that lag actual coaching interest. A 3-star recruit who fits a specific scheme and fills a roster need is more likely to receive an offer than a 4-star who does not match the program's system.

*Strategic opening:* Build a proprietary fit score that is scheme-aware, roster-aware, and coaching-staff-aware. Present this alongside (or instead of) star ratings. Over time, if Alex Recruiting's fit scores predict offers more accurately than star ratings, that becomes the platform's moat.

**Orthodoxy 5: "The recruiting process starts junior year."**
Most families treat freshman and sophomore year as "too early." The reality is that relationship-building, film accumulation, and athletic development data begin compounding from day one. Coaches evaluate trajectory, not snapshots. A recruit who demonstrates four years of consistent improvement tells a fundamentally different story than one who appears for the first time at a junior-year camp.

*Strategic opening:* Alex Recruiting already has a head start here. The longitudinal data architecture is the most defensible asset in the system. v3 should make this explicit: "Your recruiting story started freshman year. Here is the proof."

### Lens 2: Trends

**Trend 1: AI is transforming the coach's side of recruiting.**
Tools like PortalIntel.AI are giving coaching staffs AI-powered scouting and evaluation capabilities. Sports Illustrated reported in February 2026 that AI in recruiting is now about processing information faster, not replacing judgment. This means coaches will increasingly expect structured, data-rich profiles rather than glossy marketing materials. The recruit who presents information in a format optimized for coach consumption wins.

**Trend 2: NIL is reshaping the economics of commitment.**
Name, Image, and Likeness (NIL) has introduced a financial dimension to recruiting that did not exist before 2021. By the time Jacob is a junior (2028-29), NIL valuation will be sophisticated, with marketplace dynamics, collective negotiations, and agent-like intermediaries. The platform needs an NIL intelligence layer that estimates market value by position, school, and conference.

**Trend 3: The transfer portal has made commitment reversible.**
Approximately 2,000+ football players entered the transfer portal in the 2024-25 cycle. This fundamentally changes the commitment decision -- it is no longer permanent. The platform should acknowledge this reality: help the family make the best initial decision AND prepare for the possibility that the portal becomes relevant.

**Trend 4: Social media platform fragmentation continues.**
X/Twitter remains important for football recruiting (coaches are active there), but Instagram, TikTok, and Threads each serve different functions. The content engine needs to be multi-platform with format-native content (short-form video for TikTok, carousel graphics for Instagram, text-heavy analysis for X).

**Trend 5: AI regulation may constrain automated outreach.**
As AI-generated messaging becomes more common, both platforms and regulators may impose restrictions on automated outreach. The platform should build human-in-the-loop approval as a core feature, not an afterthought, to stay ahead of potential restrictions.

### Lens 4: Discontinuities (Potential Disruptions)

**Discontinuity 1: NCAA rule changes on contact periods.**
The NCAA has been trending toward deregulation of recruiting contact rules. If the June 15 contact date moves earlier (or is eliminated), the platform's value proposition shifts -- early relationship-building becomes even more critical, and the intelligence engine needs to handle higher volumes of coach interaction sooner.

**Discontinuity 2: A major recruiting platform adds AI.**
If Hudl, NCSA, or 247Sports ships a competitive AI-powered recruiting assistant, the window for differentiation narrows. Alex Recruiting's defense is depth of integration and longitudinal data -- surface-level AI features are easy to replicate, but four years of compounded intelligence is not.

**Discontinuity 3: Social media platform policy changes.**
If X/Twitter restricts API access further (as it has been trending since the Musk acquisition), or if DM automation triggers account suspensions, the outreach engine needs platform-agnostic fallbacks (email, direct contact forms, phone).

**Discontinuity 4: NIL collective collapse or regulation.**
If Congress passes NIL legislation that restructures the current collective model, the financial analysis layer needs to adapt. Build the NIL intelligence as a modular component, not deeply coupled to current rules.

**Discontinuity 5: AI-generated film manipulation.**
As AI video tools improve, there is a risk that recruits begin using AI to enhance or fabricate film. This could trigger a backlash against digital film and increase the value of verified, platform-authenticated film with tamper-proof timestamps.

### Lens 5: Unmet Needs

#### What Recruits Actually Need (But Nobody Provides)

**Unmet Need 1: Emotional regulation support.**
The recruiting process is a 4-year psychological gauntlet (Flow's term). Recruits experience anxiety, impostor syndrome, identity confusion, comparison pressure, and decision paralysis. No recruiting platform addresses the emotional experience. The platform should provide confidence architecture -- documented mastery moments, progress visualization, and pressure management tools.

**Unmet Need 2: A decision framework, not just information.**
When offers arrive, families are overwhelmed with unstructured data (campus culture, depth charts, coaching stability, NIL potential, academic programs, geography). They need a structured decision-making framework with weighted criteria they define themselves -- built before any offer arrives, not after.

**Unmet Need 3: Coach communication coaching.**
Recruits do not know how to talk to coaches. They do not know what questions to ask on official visits. They do not know how to express genuine interest without appearing desperate or disinterested. The AI team should actively coach the recruit on communication skills, not just automate messages.

**Unmet Need 4: Training-to-recruiting bridge.**
No platform connects what happens in the weight room to what appears on the recruiting profile. An offensive lineman who improves his squat by 100 lbs over two years while maintaining his 40 time tells a story that coaches want to see -- but that data never makes it into recruiting profiles.

**Unmet Need 5: Parent guidance.**
Parents are deeply invested but often unsure how to help without creating pressure. The platform should provide a parent layer with appropriate visibility (weekly summaries, not real-time monitoring) and guidance on how to be supportive without adding pressure.

#### What Coaches Actually Need (But Nobody Provides)

**Coach Unmet Need 1: Risk reduction data.**
Coaches are not just evaluating talent -- they are evaluating risk. Transfer portal exit rates, injury history, academic trajectory, and character signals all factor into decisions. A profile that transparently addresses these factors demonstrates maturity and reduces coaching staff anxiety.

**Coach Unmet Need 2: Scheme-specific evaluation.**
Generic film does not help a coach who runs a specific system. A zone-blocking coordinator needs to see lateral agility, hip fluidity, and reach block technique. A gap-power scheme needs to see drive blocking power and pull mechanics. Film that is tagged and presented by scheme fit is dramatically more useful.

**Coach Unmet Need 3: Quick evaluation format.**
Coaches evaluate hundreds of recruits. They do not have time for 6-minute highlight reels. The ideal format is: 90-second overview, key metrics, 5-10 tagged plays that demonstrate specific skills. If the platform delivers this, coaches come back.

### Lens 6: Analogies (Lessons From Other Industries)

**Analogy 1: Talent Acquisition (LinkedIn/Greenhouse)**
LinkedIn's profile strength indicator drives completion behavior without feeling like a game. Alex Recruiting should implement a "Recruiting Readiness Score" that shows the recruit how complete and competitive their profile is across film, measurables, academics, and outreach activity.

**Analogy 2: Dating Apps (Hinge/Bumble)**
The mutual-match concept applies directly to recruiting. Schools have needs (position, scheme, academic standards). Recruits have preferences (geography, culture, playing time). A fit engine that surfaces mutual matches -- where the recruit fits the school's needs AND the school fits the recruit's preferences -- is more valuable than a ranked list of "dream schools."

**Analogy 3: Duolingo (Streaks and Consistent Effort)**
Duolingo's streak mechanic keeps users engaged in a long-term learning journey. Recruiting is similarly a multi-year effort where consistent small actions compound. The platform should create recruiting streaks -- weekly outreach cadence, film review sessions, profile updates -- that make consistency feel rewarding.

**Analogy 4: Strava (Athletic Community + Data)**
Strava turned solo running into a social experience with segments, personal records, and community challenges. The athletic development layer of Alex Recruiting could create similar dynamics -- personal records in the weight room, training consistency streaks, measurable improvement over time.

**Analogy 5: Bloomberg Terminal (Information Advantage)**
Bloomberg sells information advantage to traders. Alex Recruiting should sell information advantage to recruiting families. The Intelligence Engine should feel like a Bloomberg terminal for recruiting -- real-time coach activity, offer probability signals, roster need alerts, and competitive intelligence that no other family has access to.

## Part 2: Future-Back Product Roadmap

### v5 (2029-2030): Senior Year -- The Commitment Engine

**Theme: "The platform understands people, not just patterns."**

Jacob is making his final commitment decision. The platform must facilitate the most important decision of his athletic career with clarity, confidence, and complete information.

**Feature 1: Commitment Decision Support System**
Multi-criteria decision analysis tool where Jacob and the family have pre-defined weighted criteria (built in v4). Schools are scored against these criteria with AI-generated analysis. The system surfaces what you will regret -- priority misalignment that is not obvious from campus visits. Framed as insight, not recommendation: "Here is what the data suggests about your priorities."

**Feature 2: Relationship Health Dashboard**
Continuous scoring of how warm each coach relationship is, with decay functions (relationship cools if 30 days of no signal). Loss-framing copy: "Your relationship with Coach Torres is cooling. Last strong signal: 18 days ago. Recommended action: [specific]." This is the behavioral economics principle (Freya) operationalized in the product.

**Feature 3: Alumni Outcome Modeling**
ML model that predicts post-college outcomes (NFL draft probability, transfer rate, playing time likelihood) for recruits matching Jacob's profile at each school. Requires 6-7 years of platform + public data to build reliably -- this is why it is a v5 feature, not v3.

**Feature 4: Official Visit Preparation Suite**
AI-generated question lists customized to each school visit based on the intelligence engine's data about that program. Pre-visit briefing on coaching staff tendencies, recent program changes, depth chart analysis. Post-visit reflection prompts that feed the decision framework.

**Feature 5: Emotional State Support**
Sentiment and behavioral model that detects when Jacob is losing confidence, disengaging, or spiraling on a decision. Triggers supportive intervention -- not push notifications, but context-appropriate encouragement based on his documented mastery history. Echo's critical constraint applies: every intervention must reduce anxiety, not harvest it.

**Dependencies:** v4's decision criteria framework must be in place. Coach personality models from v3 must be mature. Alumni outcome data requires multi-year data collection starting now.

**Risks:**
- Decision support tool could be perceived as making the choice for the family if framing is wrong
- Emotional state detection on a minor requires careful ethical guardrails
- NCAA compliance review needed for any feature that influences commitment timing

### v4 (2028-2029): Junior Year -- The Autonomous Recruiting Agent

**Theme: "The platform recruits for you."**

Junior year is when recruiting accelerates. Coaches can officially contact after June 15 of sophomore year, but the real volume hits junior year. The platform must handle high-volume communication, real offers, and active evaluation by coaching staffs.

**Feature 1: Recruiting Strategy Agent**
Goal-directed AI agent that manages the full recruiting pipeline -- sets outreach cadence, escalates or pauses based on coach signals, suggests next moves. If a coach opens 3 messages but never replies, the agent shifts strategy (different tone, CC recruiting coordinator, recommend campus visit prompt). Human approval gate for high-stakes moves (official visit requests, verbal commitment conversations).

**Feature 2: NIL and Scholarship Market Intelligence**
ML model that estimates NIL opportunity value per school by position, profile, and conference -- weighted against academic scholarship probability. By 2028, there will be 6+ years of structured NIL data. Pattern recognition becomes viable. The emotional output: Jacob feels informed, not naive, going into conversations about money.

**Feature 3: Dynamic Coach-Specific Profiles**
AI-assembled profile that dynamically weights what to show each coach based on what that coach cares about. Coach A (defensive coordinator evaluating blocking technique) sees pad level and drive block distance first. Coach B (OC running a zone scheme) sees lateral agility and hip fluidity first. Built on the coach personality models trained in v3.

**Feature 4: Offer Probability Delta Alerts**
Real-time scoring engine that detects when Jacob's offer probability at a specific school spikes (roster need opened, competing recruit decommitted, coach attended his game). Triggers immediate outreach recommendation. Target latency: alert within 4 hours of signal detection.

**Feature 5: Decision Criteria Framework Builder**
Before offers arrive, Jacob and the family define and weight their priorities: coaching stability, scheme fit, academic program, geographic proximity, depth chart opportunity, culture, NIL potential. This becomes the anchor (Freya's Bias 1 countermeasure) that prevents emotional anchoring to whichever school contacts first.

**Dependencies:** v3's coach personality profiles and fit scoring must be operational. Content engine must be multi-platform. Athletic development data from v3 must be rich enough to power dynamic profiles.

**Risks:**
- Autonomous agent must have clear guardrails to prevent unauthorized communication
- NIL intelligence accuracy depends on data quality and availability
- Offer probability models may need 5,000+ labeled examples to be reliable -- data acquisition is the bottleneck

### v3 (2027-2028): Sophomore Year -- The Foundation Layer

**Theme: "Stop guessing. Start building proof."**

Sophomore year is about accumulating the data, relationships, and film that make junior and senior year productive. The platform should make the boring foundational work feel purposeful and visible.

**Feature 1: Recruit-to-Program Fit Scoring**
ML model that scores probability of receiving an offer from each program (0-100%). Inputs: athletic measurables, academic profile, position scarcity at each school, roster graduation timelines, historical offer patterns. Target: >72% accuracy at 90-day horizon. This prevents Jacob from wasting effort on programs that will never offer and builds confidence by showing which schools are realistic targets.

**Feature 2: Athletic Development Tracker**
Longitudinal tracking of OL-specific measurables that coaches care about:
- Tier 1 (gatekeeping): height, weight, hand size, arm length, 40-yard dash, 5-10-5 shuttle, 3-cone, vertical jump, broad jump
- Tier 2 (differentiating): squat/bench/clean progression curves, power-to-weight ratio over time, body composition trends
- Film metrics: pass pro success rate, drive block distance, pad level consistency, pull mechanics
- The key insight from Coach: trajectory is the asset. A recruit who enters at 240 lbs and reaches 295 with maintained athleticism metrics is more compelling than a static 295-lb snapshot.

**Feature 3: Film Intelligence Engine**
Computer vision + NLP pipeline that auto-tags game film with OL-specific metrics: kick-slide distance, hand placement height, first-step quickness, drive block yards, penalty tendencies. Generates coach-ready film summaries in the 90-second format coaches actually consume. Scheme-fit tags (zone specialist vs. gap power profile) that match film presentation to coaching staff preferences.

**Feature 4: Multi-Platform Content Engine**
Expand beyond X/Twitter to Instagram, TikTok, and Threads with format-native content:
- X: text analysis, recruiting updates, coach engagement
- Instagram: training progress carousels, campus visit stories, highlight clips
- TikTok: short-form training montages, day-in-the-life content
- Platform-specific analytics showing which content drives coach engagement

**Feature 5: Recruiting Readiness Score**
LinkedIn-inspired profile completeness indicator that shows how competitive Jacob's profile is across five dimensions: film quality, measurables data, academic profile, outreach activity, and coach engagement. This drives completion behavior without gamification fatigue and gives the family a clear answer to "are we on track?"

**Dependencies:** Data collection protocol must be established immediately (quarterly anthropometrics, bi-annual combine testing, monthly 1RM logging, film tagging per season). X API access must remain stable. Film analysis requires partnership with or access to game film recording.

**Risks:**
- Fit scoring model needs training data that may not exist yet in sufficient volume
- Film computer vision is computationally expensive and may require cloud processing costs
- Multi-platform content creation increases operational complexity significantly

## Part 3: Behavioral Architecture

### Cognitive Biases to Design Against (Freya)

| Bias | How It Manifests in Recruiting | Platform Countermeasure |
|------|-------------------------------|----------------------|
| **Anchoring** | First offer/visit sets the reference point for all subsequent evaluations | Pre-anchor with data comparison matrix BEFORE first visit |
| **Status Quo Bias** | Families resist decommitting even when better options arise | "What Changed" alerts that frame new data as a new decision, not a reversal |
| **Hyperbolic Discounting** | Coaches weaponize artificial deadlines ("decide by Friday") | Deadline Decoder: cross-reference historical data on how often offers are actually pulled |
| **IKEA Effect** | Effort invested in a coaching relationship inflates perceived program value | Relationship Audit Tool separating relationship intensity from fit quality |
| **Social Proof** | Peer commits and star ratings distort the decision | Identity Forward framing toward long-term outcomes, not current status signals |

### Habit Formation Architecture (Echo)

Echo flagged a critical constraint: **Jacob is a minor. The same mechanisms that build healthy habits can create anxiety, compulsive checking, and identity distortion in adolescent brains.** Every habit engineered must reduce anxiety, not harvest it.

**Year-by-Year Engagement Design:**

| Year | Habit Goal | Weekly Target | Daily Target | Hook Mechanism |
|------|-----------|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Freshman (14)** | Build the log habit | 1 Sunday reflection (5 min max) | None -- do not over-trigger | "Your freshman year story is building" timeline |
| **Sophomore (15)** | Add film review + college list | 1 post-game reflection + 1 film tag | Optional "college of the day" card | "Your strongest play type this month" insights |
| **Junior (16)** | Weekly outreach + film submission | 1 outreach action + 1 film update | 5-min inbox check, CAPPED | "Your recruiting week" Monday brief |
| **Senior (17-18)** | Shift from building to deciding | 1 shortlist review + 1 journal entry | Zero -- a good decision is the goal | "Look how far you've come" 4-year retrospective |

**Critical Design Principle:** The platform should feel less like a recruiting tracker and more like a personal coach who remembers everything and believes in you. The emotional target at every touchpoint: calm confidence, not anxious monitoring.

**Parent Visibility Layer:** Separate, lower-frequency parent view (weekly summary only) to prevent over-monitoring from adding pressure. The recruit owns the platform. Parents observe, they do not operate it.

## Part 4: Growth Strategy

### Viral Growth Loops (Aria)

**Loop 1: Recruiting Card Share (Priority: Highest)**
Recruit builds AI profile -> platform generates a shareable "Recruiting Card" (stats, highlights, fit scores) -> recruit shares to social media -> other recruits see it -> they sign up. The card footer reads "Built with Alex Recruiting AI" -- non-removable on free tier. Target: 15-20% of profile completions result in a share. Each share should drive 2-4 profile views.

**Loop 2: Commitment Announcement (Priority: High)**
When Jacob commits, the platform generates a branded announcement graphic showing the AI match score and a "look back" showing the platform predicted this fit. Every commitment announcement in football recruiting is a cultural moment with enormous organic reach. Insert the platform into a ritual that already has massive energy.

**Loop 3: Team Cluster Effect (Priority: High)**
One recruit signs up -> platform identifies their school -> prompts "3 of your teammates are already building profiles" -> loss framing drives teammate signups. Reward is recruiting-relevant (unlock AI film analysis), not generic (no gift cards). Target: 15% organic penetration per team, referral pushes to 35-40%.

**Loop 4: Coach Outreach Loop (Priority: Medium)**
Recruits send AI-personalized outreach packets to coaches -> coaches are impressed -> coaches recommend the platform to other recruits. Requires a coach-side product to close the loop fully. This is a product dependency to resolve.

**Loop 5: Parent Community Loop (Priority: Medium)**
Parents share milestone content in Facebook groups, booster clubs, and local sports communities. Create a "Recruiting Journey Timeline" export designed to be printed, framed, and posted. Physical artifacts create word-of-mouth that digital-only products miss.

## Part 5: Gamification System

### Progression Mechanics (Quest)

**Mechanic 1: Recruit Rating Progression**
Tiered profile rating (Prospect -> Contender -> Priority Target -> Blue Chip) based on completed recruiting activities. Each tier unlocks platform features. Uses identity language so recruits see who they are becoming, not just what they have done.

**Mechanic 2: Recruiting Quest Board**
Structured milestone checklist framed as missions: "First Contact" (log first coach email), "Film Ready" (upload 3 clips), "Campus Scout" (log a visit), "The Offer" (log an offer). Imposes narrative structure on a chaotic process. The journey feels like progress, not chaos.

**Mechanic 3: Recruiting Streak System**
Weekly engagement streak tied to real recruiting activity. Loss framing: "Coach Martinez viewed your profile 3 days ago. No follow-up logged. Recruits who respond within 48 hours hear back 2x more often." Streak resets after 72 hours of no activity.

**Mechanic 4: School Comparison Board**
Private ranked board where schools are scored by personalized fit (recruit sets weights). Schools move based on new information. "Your #1 fit hasn't heard from you in 14 days" triggers specific action. Creates agency: the recruit feels like the chooser, not the chosen.

**Mechanic 5: Milestone Badges with Social Share**
Auto-generated shareable cards for key moments ("First D1 Offer," "Official Visit Locked In," "Top 10 Named"). Sharing earns XP back into progression system. Schools can see which recruits are publicly posting about them -- a soft signal of interest.

## Part 6: Sports Psychology Framework

### The 5 Principles That Matter (Flow)

**Principle 1: Identity Stability Under External Evaluation**
The recruit must anchor identity to process commitments, not offer status. "I am the guy who watches film every Sunday" is durable. "I am a 4-star recruit" is fragile. Platform copy, achievement names, and progress tracking should reinforce process identity.

**Principle 2: Confidence Architecture**
Build a confidence inventory -- documented mastery moments (specific plays, weight PRs, technique improvements). When confidence dips, this is the reference document, not the offer list. The platform should separate evaluation events from confidence events.

**Principle 3: Micro-Commitment Consistency**
Written commitments to specific process goals each semester ("complete film review every Sunday for 16 weeks"). Each honored commitment reinforces the self-narrative: "I do what I say I will do." This matters enormously when a coach asks "are you committed to this program?"

**Principle 4: Pressure Management**
Teach physiological reappraisal: "My heart is racing because my body is preparing me, not because I am in danger." Practice evaluation simulation in low-stakes environments. Reframe official visits as bilateral evaluation -- Jacob is evaluating them too.

**Principle 5: Decision-Making Under Uncertainty**
Build a decision criteria document before any offer arrives. When a program creates an artificial deadline, the criteria document is the defense. Satisficing (choosing when criteria are met) outperforms optimizing (endlessly searching for the perfect fit) under high uncertainty.

**4-Year Psychological Calendar:**

| Year | Primary Focus | Key Practice |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| Freshman | Identity anchoring | Written process commitments; confidence inventory started |
| Sophomore | Confidence architecture | Mastery documentation; pressure simulation begins |
| Junior | Pressure management | Evaluation reframing; decision criteria document built |
| Senior | Decision-making | Commitment psychology applied; post-decision consistency |

**Tier 2 -- Scheme-Dependent Differentiators:**

| Metric | Zone Blocking | Gap/Power |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Lateral agility emphasis | High | Moderate |
| Raw strength (squat/bench) | Moderate | High |
| Pad level at contact | Critical | Critical |
| Footwork precision | High | High |

**Longitudinal Data Architecture:**

```
FRESHMAN BASELINE
  - Anthropometrics: height, weight, wingspan, hand size
  - Movement screen: FMS composite score
  - Strength baseline: squat, bench, hang clean 1RM
  - Film: stance, initial contact, pull mechanics

SOPHOMORE LAYER
  - Growth delta: height/weight change rate
  - Strength progression curve (% gain vs. baseline)
  - Combine metrics: first recorded shuttle, 40, jumps
  - Film: technique evolution markers

JUNIOR LAYER
  - Peak power output (force plate if available)
  - Injury history + recovery timeline
  - Scheme versatility: zone vs. gap reps logged
  - Velocity-based training output if tracked

SENIOR LAYER
  - Final combine metrics + percentile vs. national pool
  - 4-year strength progression curve
  - Game film analytics: pass pro success rate, run block grades
  - Full development trajectory visualization
```

**The key insight:** Trajectory is the asset. A recruit who demonstrates consistent upward development across multiple data streams is a lower-risk signing -- and risk reduction is the emotional core of what college coaches are buying.

**Film Analysis Metrics to Auto-Tag:**

Pass Protection:
- Rep success rate (clean pocket >= 2.5 seconds)
- Kick-slide distance per rep
- Hand placement height consistency
- Recovery step frequency after broken contact

Run Blocking:
- First-step quickness (frames to initial contact post-snap)
- Pad level at contact
- Drive block distance (yards defender displaced)
- Pull angle and lead block target acquisition rate

## Part 9: Bold / Contrarian Ideas

### Ideas That Could Differentiate Alex Recruiting From Everything Else

**1. The Anti-Highlights Platform**
Instead of helping recruits build better highlight reels (which coaches discount), build a platform that presents full, unedited game film with AI-tagged plays. Market this as "what coaches actually want to see." This is contrarian because every recruiting service sells highlights. The insight: coaches trust transparency more than marketing.

**2. The Recruit Evaluates The School**
Flip the power dynamic. Instead of "will this school offer me?", build tools for "should I want this school?" -- coaching staff stability scores, player development ratings, transfer portal exit rates, academic outcomes by major. This reframes the recruit as a buyer, not a seller. It also builds the kind of informed, confident recruit that coaches prefer to recruit.

**3. The Recruiting Timeline as a Physical Artifact**
Create a printable, frameable "Recruiting Journey" document that tells the 4-year story from freshman profile creation to commitment. This becomes a family keepsake and a powerful word-of-mouth driver in booster clubs and communities. Digital products are forgettable; physical artifacts persist.

**4. Verified Development Data (The Carfax for Recruits)**
Partner with high school strength coaches to verify athletic development data. Create a "Verified" badge that indicates measurables were independently confirmed. This addresses the trust gap that coaches have with self-reported data and creates a premium feature worth paying for.

**5. The Coach Product**
Build a free dashboard for college coaches that surfaces recruits matching their program's specific needs. This creates the other side of the marketplace, gives coaches a reason to engage with the platform directly, and generates the data needed to close the coach outreach viral loop.

## Part 12: Synthesis -- What Must Be True

If Alex Recruiting succeeds through v5, these things must be true:

1. **The longitudinal data moat is real.** Four years of compounded intelligence, athletic development data, and relationship history cannot be replicated by any competitor who starts later. This is the single most defensible asset.

2. **The platform reduces anxiety, not increases it.** Every feature designed for a minor must pass Echo's test: does this create calm confidence or anxious monitoring? Features that fail this test get cut regardless of engagement metrics.

3. **Coaches find the platform useful, not annoying.** If coach-facing features (film intelligence, structured profiles, scheme-tagged evaluation) are genuinely better than what coaches currently receive, the platform earns a place in the recruiting ecosystem. If they are just more noise, the platform is another recruiter in the spam folder.

4. **The family trusts the AI team.** The 7-agent architecture must feel like a competent staff, not a chatbot with personas. Trust is built through accuracy, consistency, and transparent limitations ("we do not have enough data to be confident about this yet").

5. **Growth loops activate without paid acquisition.** The recruiting card share loop and commitment announcement loop are culturally native to football recruiting behavior. If these work, the platform grows with every recruiting cycle. If they do not, growth requires paid channels that may not be sustainable.

**The contrarian risk nobody is talking about:** The biggest threat to Alex Recruiting is not a competitor -- it is the possibility that the recruiting process itself becomes less stressful and more transparent through NCAA reform. If that happens, the anxiety-reduction value proposition loses force. The hedge: build the platform's value on information advantage and development tracking, not on navigating a broken system.
