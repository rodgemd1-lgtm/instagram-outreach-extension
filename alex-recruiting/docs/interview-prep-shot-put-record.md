## PART 2: INTERVIEW TALKING POINTS FOR JACOB

### What the Coaching Panel Says Matters

The panel coaches consistently reward **specificity over motivation**, **facts over feelings**, and **team-first language over individual hype**. This interview is a recruiting asset — it will be indexed, shared, and read by coaches. Every answer should pass the test: *"Would a college coach respect this answer?"*

---

### Q: "Tell me about the record. How did it happen?"

**What to say:**
> "I've been training year-round since I was 12 — five days a week between the weight room, my personal trainer, and NX Level for agility work. Shot put uses a lot of the same muscles I train for football — explosive hips, leg drive, core rotation. I think the training carried over. When I got to the meet, I felt prepared and just executed."

**Why:** Coaches Nordin (NDSU) and Arens (Mankato) look for specific training details and weight room consistency. Naming NX Level and the personal trainer shows this isn't accidental — it's the result of a system.

---

### Q: "How did you get into shot put / track and field?"

**What to say:**
> "My coaches encouraged me to go out for track because the throwing events complement football. Discus and shot put are all about hip explosion and finishing through contact — the same things I work on as a lineman. I wanted to compete in another sport and keep developing in the offseason."

**Why:** Coach Janus (UW-Eau Claire) values coachability. Saying "my coaches encouraged me" signals Jacob listens to his coaches. Coach Keel (Whitewater) looks for technical vocabulary — "hip explosion" and "finishing through contact" are football-coach language.

---

### Q: "What does your training look like?"

**What to say:**
> "Right now I train five days a week. I work with a personal trainer on compound lifts — bench, squat, deadlift — all tailored for offensive and defensive line. I also go to NX Level for agility and footwork. During track season I add throwing practice on top of that. I've logged over 730 sessions since I started at 12."

**Key numbers to have ready:**
- Bench: 265 lbs
- Squat: 350 lbs
- Deadlift: 405 lbs (PR)
- Training sessions: 730+
- Training age: Started at 12 (3+ years)

**Why:** Coach Nerat (Stevens Point) values consistency and progress tracking. Coach Raehl (Oshkosh) wants to see measurable progress over time. The 730 sessions number is concrete evidence of discipline.

---

### Q: "You also play football — what position?"

**What to say:**
> "I play both sides of the ball — offensive guard and defensive tackle. Last season as a freshman I started on both JV and varsity. I had 11 pancake blocks, 3 sacks, and a fumble recovery. Our team made a deep playoff run. I'm 6'4", 285 right now, and I'm looking to keep developing for college."

**Why:** Coach Cebulski (Winona State) looks for team mentions over individual stats. Leading with "our team made a deep playoff run" before personal stats is the right order. Mentioning both positions shows versatility — something every panel coach flagged as a differentiator.

---

### Q: "What are your goals?"

**What to say:**
> "Short term, I want to keep improving my marks in shot put and discus and help our track team compete. For football, I'm focused on getting stronger and adding to my film this fall. Long term, I want to play college football. I'm starting to build my school list and I'm looking for a program with a strong line tradition and a coaching staff that develops players."

**Why:** Coach Nordin (NDSU) looks for championship mindset and accountability language. Coach Arens (Mankato) values drive to improve. This answer shows both short-term focus and long-term vision without sounding rehearsed.

---

### Q: "What drives you to put in the work?"

**What to say:**
> "I just love competing. Whether it's football, track, or the weight room — I want to get better every day. I started training at 12 and I haven't missed a scheduled session since October 2022. Once you build that habit, it's just who you are."

**Why:** Coach Schmidt (SDSU) values authentic voice and personality. This answer is honest and personal without being a cliche. Avoid: "I'm built different," "nobody outworks me," "grind season." The panel has consistently flagged those phrases as turnoffs.
